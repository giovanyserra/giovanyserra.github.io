Robust IQ Imbalance Compensation for Free Space Optics Communications Links

Abstract:

In this paper, we propose a robust IQ compensation scheme for optical communication links, which consists of two compensation stages. Through targeted simulations and experimental measurements, we demonstrate that the proposed approach achieves an improvement of over 1 dB in signal-to-noise ratio (SNR) compared to typical single-stage processing. This double-stage IQ imbalance compensation effectively addresses phase and amplitude imbalance effects that degrade the performance of coherent optical communication systems. By mitigating the imbalances introduced by the receiver front-end and the transmitter modulator, our scheme enhances overall system performance. The improvement in SNR underscores the robustness and reliability of our approach, making it well-suited for high-demand optical communication networks.

Universal Bias Controller Testbed for DP-IQ Modulators in Coherent Optical Links

Abstract:

This paper presents the design, implementation, and testing of a testbed for the automatic bias controller (ABC) required in dual-polarization in-phase and quadrature (DP-IQ) modulators, like those used in coherent optical communication systems. The importance of controlling and adjusting the modulator bias signals is analyzed, as they directly impact the SNR of the demodulated signal. The operation of the testbed is demonstrated by implementing a bias adjustment algorithm that uses auxiliary signals or dithers. Finally, the performance of the testbed and ABC is experimentally validated by implementing a coherent optical communication system with DP-QPSK modulation at 10, 20, 100, and 128Gb/s data rates.

Implementation of a 10 Gbps Coherent Receiver for Free-Space Optical Communications

Abstract:

We show the implementation of an optical coherent receiver suitable for inter-satellite communications supporting both Dual-Polarization Binary Phase Shift Keying (DP-BPSK) and Quadrature Phase Shift Keying (DP-QPSK) modulation formats, enabling data transmission at rates of up to 10 Gbps. The performance of the implemented receiver's digital signal processing algorithms is numerically and experimentally evaluated under different operation conditions and channel impairments, such as received power, carrier frequency offset, laser linewidth, and polarization rotation.
